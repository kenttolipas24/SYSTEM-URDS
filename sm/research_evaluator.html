Research Proposal

AI-Driven Traffic Management System

UNIVERSITY OF EASTERN PHILIPPINES

University Town, Northern Samar

UNIVERSITY RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT SERVICES

RESEARCH PROPOSAL

UEP-URDS-FM-003

I. BASIC INFORMATION
Research Title: AI-Driven Traffic Management System
Project Leader: Dr. Michael Chen, Ph.D.
Team Members: Prof. Sarah Johnson (Computer Vision Specialist)
Dr. Robert Lee (Machine Learning Expert)
Engr. Maria Santos (IoT Systems Engineer)
Department/College: College of Engineering - Computer Engineering Department
Research Classification: Applied Research
Location: University Town, Northern Samar and Metro Manila Test Sites
Duration: 24 months (January 2026 - December 2027)
Total Budget: PHP 2,500,000.00
II. TECHNICAL INFORMATION
Background and Rationale: Traffic congestion in urban areas has become a critical challenge affecting economic productivity, environmental sustainability, and quality of life. Traditional traffic management systems rely on fixed-time signals and manual intervention, which are inefficient in handling dynamic traffic patterns. This research proposes an AI-driven traffic management system that uses computer vision, machine learning, and IoT sensors to optimize traffic flow in real-time, reduce congestion, and improve overall urban mobility.
Research Objectives: 1. Develop a computer vision system for real-time vehicle detection and traffic density analysis
2. Design machine learning algorithms for predictive traffic pattern modeling
3. Create an adaptive traffic signal control system based on real-time data
4. Implement IoT sensor networks for comprehensive traffic monitoring
5. Evaluate system performance in reducing congestion and travel time
III. REVIEW OF RELATED LITERATURE
Recent studies in intelligent transportation systems (Zhang et al., 2024) demonstrate that AI-based traffic management can reduce congestion by up to 35% compared to traditional systems. Deep learning approaches, particularly convolutional neural networks, have shown 95% accuracy in vehicle detection (Kumar & Wang, 2023). Reinforcement learning algorithms have been successfully applied to optimize traffic signal timing (Li et al., 2024), achieving 28% reduction in average wait times. IoT sensor integration provides comprehensive data collection for traffic prediction models (Santos & Rodriguez, 2023).
IV. METHODOLOGY
Research Design: Experimental research with mixed-methods approach
Data Collection Methods: - High-resolution cameras for video capture
- IoT sensors for vehicle counting and speed measurement
- GPS data from mobile applications
- Historical traffic data from local traffic management offices
Implementation Phases: Phase 1 (Months 1-6): System design and algorithm development
Phase 2 (Months 7-12): Prototype implementation and laboratory testing
Phase 3 (Months 13-18): Field deployment and pilot testing
Phase 4 (Months 19-24): Performance evaluation and optimization
Analysis Techniques: Statistical analysis of traffic flow metrics, machine learning model evaluation, comparative analysis with baseline systems
V. EXPECTED OUTPUTS AND DELIVERABLES
1. Functional AI-driven traffic management system prototype
2. Published research papers in peer-reviewed journals (minimum 2)
3. Technical documentation and system architecture
4. Training materials for traffic management operators
5. Policy recommendations for local government implementation
